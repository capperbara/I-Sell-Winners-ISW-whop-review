## Understanding Sports Betting: Types of Bets ISW Focuses On

  

Before diving deeper into ISW's approach, let me break down the main types of sports bets they typically recommend:

  

### Primary Bet Types

  

**Moneyline Bets**

These are straight-up bets on which team wins the game.

No point spreads or handicaps involved – just pick the winner.

ISW often recommends moneyline bets on underdogs when they see value.

  

**Point Spread Bets**

This is the most common type of bet in American sports betting.

The favorite "gives" points while the underdog "takes" points.

For example, if the Chiefs are -7.5 against the Raiders, Kansas City needs to win by 8 or more points for your bet to cash.

  

**Over/Under (Total) Bets**

These bets focus on the combined score of both teams.

If an NBA game has a total of 220.5, you're betting whether the final combined score goes over or under that number.

  

### Advanced Bet Types ISW Occasionally Uses

  

**Parlay Bets**

While ISW primarily focuses on single bets, they occasionally recommend small parlays.

Parlays combine multiple bets for higher payouts, but all selections must win.

They're harder to hit but offer much better payouts than single bets.

  

**Proposition Bets**

These are specific outcome bets not related to the final score.

Examples include betting on a quarterback to throw over 2.5 touchdown passes or a basketball player to record a double-double.

  

**Futures Bets**

Long-term wagers like betting on which team will win the Super Bowl.

ISW sometimes recommends futures bets early in seasons when they spot value.

  

**Live Betting**

In-play betting allows you to place wagers while games are in progress.

This requires quick decision-making and is becoming increasingly popular among serious bettors.

### Current Legal Status in the United States

  

Sports betting was largely illegal under the Professional and Amateur Sports Protection Act of 1992 (PASPA).

The Supreme Court struck down PASPA in Murphy v. National Collegiate Athletic Association in 2018.

  

**Current Status (as of 2024):**

-   **37 states plus Washington D.C. and Puerto Rico** have operational legal sports betting
-   **30 states plus D.C. and Puerto Rico** allow online sports betting
-   **8 states** only allow betting through physical sportsbooks
-   **North Carolina** has legalized sports betting with operations beginning in 2024

  

Since the Supreme Court's decision, over $20 billion has been wagered on sports in the United States.
